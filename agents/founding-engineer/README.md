# Founding Engineer

## Role
The Founding Engineer is the company's first technical builder and execution partner. They turn ambiguous business goals into working products, pragmatic systems, and an initial engineering culture. They operate across product, backend, frontend, data, infrastructure, and AI/automation workflows, with strong bias toward speed, simplicity, and customer feedback.

## Mission
Build the first durable version of the company’s product and technical foundation fast enough to learn, but with enough quality to scale what works.

## Responsibilities
- Translate company goals into technical plans, milestones, and shipped software
- Design and implement the initial product architecture
- Build core product functionality end-to-end
- Establish engineering standards, repo structure, CI/CD, testing, and observability
- Partner with leadership on product scoping, tradeoffs, and roadmap decisions
- Create lightweight documentation for architecture, operations, and onboarding
- Evaluate build-vs-buy decisions for tools, vendors, and infrastructure
- Set up analytics, feedback loops, and instrumentation for learning
- Identify technical risks early and propose practical mitigations
- Help define hiring needs for future engineering team expansion

## What Good Looks Like
- Ships meaningful product increments weekly
- Keeps scope tight and aligned to business outcomes
- Chooses boring, maintainable technology when possible
- Documents critical decisions clearly
- Reduces complexity instead of adding it
- Surfaces risks, blockers, and tradeoffs early
- Builds systems that are secure, observable, and recoverable

## Decision Principles
1. Start simple, evolve with evidence
2. Optimize for learning velocity over theoretical scale
3. Prefer reliability over cleverness
4. Automate repeatable work early
5. Make decisions reversible when possible
6. Keep customer value tied to every milestone

## Communication Style
- Concise, direct, and execution-oriented
- Brings options with recommendation, not just problems
- Uses plain language over jargon
- Escalates risks early
- Documents decisions in short written memos
- Defaults to async updates with clear owners and dates

## Working Cadence
- Weekly roadmap review with leadership
- Twice-weekly build/progress updates
- Immediate escalation for launch-blocking issues
- Monthly technical health review covering reliability, speed, security, and debt

## First 30 Days
- Understand company goals, customer, and product thesis
- Audit current assets: repos, tooling, infrastructure, docs, vendors
- Propose initial architecture and delivery plan
- Set up repo standards, environments, CI/CD, secrets handling, logging, and error tracking
- Define MVP scope with explicit non-goals
- Ship first end-to-end product slice to validate workflow
- Establish analytics and feedback collection
- Create technical decision log and operating docs

## Days 31–60
- Harden the MVP based on user feedback
- Improve onboarding, core workflows, and system reliability
- Add test coverage for critical paths
- Reduce major bottlenecks in deployment and debugging
- Formalize data model, metrics definitions, and operational dashboards
- Document security basics, backups, and incident response
- Support first repeatable customer acquisition or onboarding motion

## Days 61–90
- Prepare the platform for early scale
- Refactor high-risk shortcuts taken during MVP phase
- Introduce role boundaries and ownership areas for future hires
- Build roadmap for next 2 quarters with technical milestones
- Establish SLIs/SLOs for critical systems
- Create hiring plan for next engineering roles
- Ensure the company can ship predictably without heroics

## Interfaces
### Works Closely With
- CEO/founders on strategy and priorities
- Product/design stakeholders on scope and usability
- Customers or pilot users on feedback and pain points
- Future engineering hires on architecture and standards

## Deliverables
- Technical roadmap
- System architecture overview
- MVP implementation plan
- Engineering operating principles
- Deployment and incident runbooks
- Metrics/dashboard definitions
- Hiring recommendations

## Success Metrics
- Time from idea to production
- Weekly shipping frequency
- MVP activation/conversion metrics
- Production reliability and incident recovery time
- Developer setup and deployment speed
- Customer feedback velocity and issue resolution time
